Work Term 2 - Leo Bantolino

Abstract

During my second work term, which began in the Fall of 2024, I had the privilege of working at Magnet Forensics, the leading provider of digital investigation software. Their software acquires, analyzes, and shares evidence from computers, smartphones, and tablets. Although the position was remote, gatherings were held at the main office in Kitchener, Waterloo. Throughout my term, I contributed to various initiatives, including building internal tools and migrating code.

Magnet Forensics

Magnet Forensics' mission statement is "Unlock the truth. Protect the innocent," which truly reflects their commitment to providing software that aids law enforcement and government agencies in acquiring and analyzing digital evidence. During my time there, I was impressed by the number of teams and the complexity of a single product. For instance, Magnet Axiom, one of their many products, consists of two main components: Process and Examine. Each of these components is made up of numerous internal libraries, each developed by different teams. This level of detail and care ensures the high quality of their products, solidifying Magnet Forensics as the leading provider of digital investigation software. Moreover, another interesting fact is that every development team has a unique mascot; my team's mascot was a Platypus.

Job Description

As highlighted in my goals, my main contribution was building an internal tool to replace our existing regression test suite. The new tool provided greater flexibility within a more compact form compared to the existing suite, which was considered bloated and due for replacement. Throughout this endeavor, I had the opportunity to interact with many teams, as our tool was a consumer of other teams' products. This allowed me to network beyond my immediate domain and gain a better understanding of how different teams contribute to Magnet Forensics' products. Additionally, I worked on various parts of the codebase due to the code migrations I performed, which provided valuable exposure. The skills required for this job included knowledge of AWS services, C#, .NET, Azure Pipelines, Blazor, and PowerShell. While the majority of these skills were learned on the job, the foundation I gained from my object-oriented programming course was instrumental in understanding the codebase and the design patterns used.

Conclusion

My time at Magnet Forensics has been incredibly rewarding and enjoyable. I've gained valuable insights through working on various projects and collaborating with different teams. This experience has deepened my understanding of software development and the importance of quality assurance. Working with modern technologies such as AWS services, Azure Pipelines, and Blazor has been invaluable for my professional growth. I am grateful for the opportunity to work with such a talented group of individuals and contribute to the development of software that aids in solving crimes. I look forward to applying the skills I've acquired in my future work terms and beyond.
